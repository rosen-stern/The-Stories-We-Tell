Stories are all we have.

But online, things have become very standardized.

We get notifications,

read a post,

and share articles and videos.

Always in the same linear way,

with margins and padding.

Almost always in the same font.

But how might our stories look if we expressed them as freely on the internet as we do to each other?

What would an oral story look like in 1s and 0s?

What if stories rained down your web page?

Or were hidden – in the dark – to be found – by you?

What if we could include the context around that photo, that memory,

to pass on to others?

What if the code below

was written in something other than

the English-centric coding languages today?

Our worldviews are shaped by the matrices of our communities.

But when our community is the internet, where does our worldview come from?

The truths we're recording on the internet may be different

depending on who is writing the story

and for whom.

And there's more to life than "objective truth",

for capturing all known knowledge.

What might our digital community be like if we wanted more than just knowledge?

What if we wanted experience?

Or reciprocity?

How would we understand the world?

How would we understand each other better?

Stories are built on stories

are built on stories

are built on stories.

They're all we are.
